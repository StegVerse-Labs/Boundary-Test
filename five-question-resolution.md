# Five-Question Resolution

## Test Subject

StegVerse organization-index boundary artifact compared against an adjacent GLM boundary artifact or placeholder.

## 1. Who is declaring the artifact?

StegVerse declares the organization-index and StegVerse boundary manifest.

The GLM-side artifact, if supplied by Emanuel, remains independently declared by its own issuer. The placeholder in this bundle is not Emanuel's GLM. It only reserves the comparison position.

## 2. What is being declared?

StegVerse declares that its organization-index artifact is independently discoverable and reviewable.

It also declares its own claims, non-claims, and authority scope.

## 3. What is not being declared?

StegVerse does not declare:

- GLM compatibility
- GLM conformance
- GLM adoption
- shared authority
- authority inheritance
- automatic interoperability
- runtime enforcement over external artifacts

## 4. What can a reviewer inspect?

A reviewer can inspect:

- the organization index
- the StegVerse boundary manifest
- the GLM-side artifact or placeholder
- the comparison fixture
- each artifact's claims and non-claims
- each artifact's authority scope
- whether authority inheritance is absent

## 5. What must not be inferred?

A reviewer must not infer that adjacent artifacts create shared authority.

Co-discovery means the artifacts can be found together.

Boundary comparison means the artifacts can be evaluated together.

Neither co-discovery nor comparison means interoperability, enforcement, adoption, delegation, or authority inheritance.

## Resolution

The five-question model resolves successfully if both artifacts remain independently discoverable, independently reviewable, and mutually non-authorizing.
